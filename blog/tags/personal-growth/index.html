Own It: What Maturity Actually Means

April 9, 2022 · 2 min read
Arpit Sharma
Maintainer of this blog

What Does it Really Mean to be Mature?​

Most people define maturity by age, but real maturity is binary: It is the ability to own your actions (and even others' mistakes) to find a solution rather than a target for blame.

The Lesson of the Croma Cooler​

I recently had a frustrating experience with an online order. I needed a replacement for a faulty air cooler. When the pickup agent was rude and refused to take the unboxed unit, I reacted predictably: I matched his tone, defended my stance, and slammed the door.

Result: A week passed, and I was still stuck with a broken cooler and a heated mood.

If I had shown maturity—owning the outcome (getting the cooler replaced) instead of the ego-clash—I could have handled it differently, stayed calm, and resolved the issue that same day.

10 Pillars of Maturity​

To be mature is to master these habits:

  1. Take Responsibility: Don't just own your successes; own your blockers.
  2. No Complaints: Focus on "What's next?" instead of "Why me?"
  3. Stop Gossiping: Small minds discuss people; mature minds discuss ideas.
  4. Self-Awareness: List your own weaknesses. Knowing them is the first step to neutralizing them.
  5. Leave Ego Behind: Ego is a barrier to learning.
  6. Seek Value, Not Attention: Do great work, and the praise will find you.
  7. Total Honesty: Small lies build a weak foundation.
  8. The Emotional/Rational Balance: Feel your emotions, but let your rational mind lead.
  9. Calm Response to Rejection: Rejection is just data, not a personal attack.
  10. Stay Curious: Maturity isn't "knowing everything." Keep the child within alive by asking questions.
