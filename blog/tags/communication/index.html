Drop the Labels: How to Connect with Anyone

December 30, 2020 · 2 min read
Arpit Sharma
Maintainer of this blog

Connection Over Classification​

When you meet new people, your brain naturally tries to label them—where they are from, what they do, or how they look. But these labels are barriers. To truly connect, you must Drop the Labels.

1. Acceptance Without Barriers​

We all start as children without biases. As we grow older, we learn to notice differences. But if you walk into a room with an open mind, animals and humans alike will feel the connection immediately. Ego is the wall; love is the bridge.

2. The 110% Rule​

Give 110% of your attention when talking to anyone. People remember how you made them feel more than what you said. Everybody fundamentally wants to be heard, respected, and understood.

3. The Communication Breakdown​

Remember that communication is far more than just words:

  • 7% Words: What you actually say.
  • 38% Tone: Your inflection and pitch.
  • 55% Body Language: Your face, hands, and posture.

4. Practical Connection Tips:​

  • Simplify Your Language: Avoid bookish jargon. Good communication is being understood by a 4th grader.
  • Master the 50-70 Rule: Maintain eye contact for 50-70% of the time to show confidence and interest.
  • Manage Your Pitch: High pitch shows nervousness; too low can feel aggressive. Find the calm middle ground.
  • Eliminate "Failure Words": Stop using filler words like "hmm," "obviously," and "you know." Silence is better than a filler.
