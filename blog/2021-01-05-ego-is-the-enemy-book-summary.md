## Ego is the Enemy - The Three Stages of Life

Ryan Holiday argues that at any given time, we are in one of three stages: **Aspire, Success, or Failure**. In each stage, ego is the enemy that prevents us from moving forward.

### 1. Aspire
When you are starting (e.g., preparing for an exam or launching a project), ego wants you to *talk* more than you *work*. 
*   **Silence is Rare**: Speaking about your goals releases a premature sense of accomplishment. Waste your energy on the action, not the announcement.
*   **Document Your Work**: Henry Ford once said, "You can't build a reputation based on what you *are going* to do."

### 2. Success
Success is often where the real danger begins. It makes us believe we are "special" or "above" the rules.
*   **Keep Your Identity Small**: The more you identify with your fame, title, or property, the more you have to lose.
*   **Stay a Student**: The moment you think you "know it all," you stop growing. If you can't swallow your pride, you can't lead.

### 3. Failure
Failure is inevitable, but how you react to it defines you.
*   **Alive Time vs. Dead Time**: "Dead time" is when you are passive and waiting for things to change. "Alive time" is when you are utilizing every second of the failure to learn and prepare for the next round.
*   **Sweeping the Floor**: Legend has it that the floor needs to be swept every single day. Similarly, your talent and mindset need daily practice, or they will gather dust.
