Decision Making Under Ambiguity

January 17, 2025 · 3 min read
Arpit Sharma
Maintainer of this blog

Read This​

A lot of people think senior leadership means having better answers.

Not exactly.

Many times it just means you have to decide before the answer becomes obvious.

That is the real difficulty.

Not decision making with full clarity. Decision making with partial information, limited time, strong opinions, and visible consequences.

That is why I think one of the most important leadership skills is this:

being able to move with imperfect information without becoming reckless.

What The Hard Thing About Hard Things Taught Me About Leading Through Pressure

Arpit Sharma
Maintainer of this blog

Read This​

Some leadership books are clean.

This one is not.

That is exactly why The Hard Thing About Hard Things works.

It does not pretend leadership is all clarity, confidence, and vision slides.

It talks about the messy part:

  • uncertainty
  • pressure
  • bad options
  • layoffs
  • underperformance
  • morale damage
  • trying to act calm when you are not calm

That is real leadership. Not the LinkedIn version of it.

Maintainer of this blog

Some leaders enter a room and make everyone sharper.

Some leaders enter a room and make everyone quieter.

That is the simplest summary of Multipliers.

This sounds simple. But in engineering leadership it is a huge difference.
